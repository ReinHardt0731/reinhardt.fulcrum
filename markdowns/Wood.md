# Aircraft Wood Cheat Sheet

---

# Common Aircraft Woods

| Wood | Well Known For | Key Characteristics | Typical Aircraft Applications |
|------|----------------|---------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Sitka Spruce** | Standard Aircraft Wood | High strength-to-weight ratio, straight grain, lightweight | Wing spars, ribs, longerons, propellers |
| **Douglas Fir** | Spruce Substitute | Strong, stiff, heavier than spruce | Spars, longerons |
| **Western Hemlock** | Spruce Alternative | Good strength, less desirable than spruce | Secondary structural members |
| **White Ash** | Shock Resistance | Tough, excellent impact resistance | Engine mounts, landing gear struts, tool handles |
| **Birch** | Aircraft Plywood | Hard, strong, fine grain | Plywood, gussets |
| **Mahogany** | Marine & Decorative Wood | Durable, rot resistant | Interior panels, propellers |
| **Balsa** | Lightest Wood | Extremely low density, good core material | Sandwich composite cores |

# Wood Defects

| Defect | Description | Effect |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| **Knots** | Embedded branch bases | Weakens the wood fibers |
| **Checks** | Surface cracks caused by drying | Reduces strength |
| **Splits** | Cracks extending completely through the wood | Serious structural defect |
| **Shakes** | Separation along annual growth rings | Weakens the grain |
| **Cross Grain** | Fibers not parallel to the length | Reduces bending strength |
| **Slope of Grain** | Grain deviates from longitudinal axis | Weakens structural members |
| **Warp** | Distortion due to uneven drying | Poor fit and alignment |
| **Decay (Rot)** | Fungal deterioration | Severe strength loss |
| **Pitch Pocket** | Resin-filled cavity | Weak point in the structure |
| **Compression Wood** | Abnormal growth in softwoods | Unstable, poor dimensional stability |
| **Worm Holes** | Insect damage | May indicate decay or weakness |

# Wood Grain Orientation

| Grain Type | Characteristics | Suitability |
|------------|----------------|-------------|
| Straight Grain | Fibers parallel to length | ✔ Best |
| Spiral Grain | Fibers spiral around trunk | ✘ Poor |
| Cross Grain | Fibers run diagonally | ✘ Weak |
| Wavy Grain | Irregular fiber direction | ✘ Not preferred |
