### Aircraft Steel Alloys - Signature Characteristics

| Alloy/Series | Well Known For | Key Characteristics | Typical Aircraft Applications |
|---------------|---------------|---------------------|-------------------------------|
| **10XX (Carbon Steel)** | General Purpose | Economical, machinable, moderate strength | Bolts, pins, hardware |
| **11XX (Free Machining Steel)** | Machinability | Sulfur improves machinability but lowers toughness | Screws, machined fittings |
| **13XX (Mn Steel)** | Wear Resistance | Stronger and harder than plain carbon steel | Gears, shafts |
| **23XX / 25XX (Nickel Steel)** | Toughness | Improved impact resistance and toughness | Structural fittings |
| **31XX / 33XX (Ni-Cr Steel)** | Hardenability | Better wear resistance and strength | Gears, shafts |
| **40XX (Moly Steel)** | Elevated Temperature Strength | Improved hardenability and creep resistance | Structural members |
| **41XX (Chrome-Moly)** | Weldability | High strength-to-weight ratio, heat treatable, easily welded | Engine mounts, welded fuselage |
| **43XX (Ni-Cr-Mo)** | Maximum Toughness | Extremely high strength and fatigue resistance | Landing gear, propeller shafts |
| **46XX / 48XX (Ni-Mo Steel)** | Fatigue Strength | Excellent toughness and hardenability | Shafts, gears |
| **51XX / 52XX (Chromium Steel)** | Bearing Steel | Excellent wear resistance | Bearings |
| **61XX (Chrome-Vanadium)** | Spring Steel | Excellent resilience and fatigue life | Springs |
| **92XX (Silicon Steel)** | Elasticity | High resilience | Springs |
| **93XX / 94XX (Gear Steel)** | Carburizing Steel | Hard surface with tough core | Aircraft gears |

---

### Aircraft Aluminum Alloys - Signature Characteristics

| Alloy/Series | Well Known For | Key Characteristics | Typical Aircraft Applications |
|---------------|---------------|---------------------|-------------------------------|
| **1XXX (Pure Aluminum)** | Corrosion Resistance & Conductivity | Excellent corrosion resistance, electrical & thermal conductivity, very ductile, non-heat treatable | Electrical conductors, chemical tanks, reflectors |
| **2XXX (Copper)** | Fatigue Resistance & Structural Strength | High strength, excellent fatigue resistance, heat treatable, poor corrosion resistance, usually Alclad | Wing skins, fuselage skins, ribs, bulkheads |
| **3XXX (Manganese)** | Formability | Moderate strength, excellent workability, good corrosion resistance, non-heat treatable | Sheet metal, fuel tanks |
| **4XXX (Silicon)** | Welding Applications | Low melting point, good wear resistance | Welding filler rods, brazing alloys, pistons |
| **5XXX (Magnesium)** | Corrosion Resistance & Weldability | Excellent corrosion resistance (especially marine), easily welded, non-heat treatable | Fuel tanks, pressure vessels |
| **6XXX (Mg-Si)** | General Purpose Structural Alloy | Good strength, excellent corrosion resistance, excellent weldability, heat treatable | Tubing, fittings, general structures |
| **7XXX (Zinc)** | Highest Strength Aluminum Alloy | Very high strength, heat treatable, good fatigue resistance, poor weldability | Wing spars, highly stressed fittings |
| **8XXX (Others)** | Specialty Alloys | Contains Li, Fe, Sn, etc. for specialized properties | Aerospace specialty applications |
