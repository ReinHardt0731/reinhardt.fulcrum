# Aircraft Composite Materials Cheat Sheet

---

# Composite Reinforcements (Fibers)

| Fiber | Well Known For | Key Characteristics | Typical Aircraft Applications |
|--------|----------------|---------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Fiberglass (Glass Fiber)** | Economical Composite | Good strength, corrosion resistant, electrically non-conductive | Fairings, radomes, interior panels |
| **Carbon Fiber (Graphite)** | Highest Strength-to-Weight Ratio | Extremely stiff, lightweight, electrically conductive | Wings, fuselage, control surfaces |
| **Kevlar (Aramid)** | Impact Resistance | Excellent toughness, abrasion resistant, difficult to machine | Leading edges, ballistic protection, radomes |
| **Boron Fiber** | High Stiffness | Extremely rigid, expensive | Military aircraft, repairs |
| **Quartz Fiber** | RF Transparency | Excellent dielectric properties | Radomes, antennas |

---

# Composite Matrix Materials

| Matrix | Well Known For | Typical Applications |
|---------|----------------|----------------------|
| **Epoxy** | Most Common Aircraft Resin | Primary aircraft composites |
| **Polyester** | Low Cost | Boats, automotive, non-critical parts |
| **Vinyl Ester** | Corrosion Resistance | Chemical tanks, marine |
| **Phenolic** | Fire Resistance | Interior panels |

---

# Core Materials

| Core | Well Known For | Typical Applications |
|------|----------------|----------------------|
| **Honeycomb** | Highest Strength-to-Weight Ratio | Wings, floors, control surfaces |
| **Foam Core** | Lightweight | Fairings, radomes |
| **Balsa Core** | Natural Sandwich Core | Older composite structures |

---

# Composite Manufacturing Methods

| Method | Remember This | Typical Applications |
|---------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Hand Lay-up** | Simplest Method | Repairs, prototypes |
| **Vacuum Bagging** | Removes Air | Aircraft repairs |
| **Autoclave Curing** | Highest Quality | Commercial aircraft |
| **Filament Winding** | Cylindrical Parts | Pressure vessels |
| **Pultrusion** | Constant Cross-Section | Beams, rods |
